## Get started with AdRecon

This guide will walk you through signing in, exploring the ad feed, saving ads, and creating your first project.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to AdRecon">
    AdRecon uses secure authentication via **Magic Link** or **Google OAuth**.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Magic Link">
        1. Navigate to your AdRecon instance
        2. Enter your email address
        3. Click **Sign in with Magic Link**
        4. Check your inbox for the authentication email
        5. Click the link in the email to sign in

        <Note>
          Magic links expire after 60 minutes and can only be used once. If you don't see the email, check your spam folder.
        </Note>
      </Tab>

      <Tab title="Google OAuth">
        1. Navigate to your AdRecon instance
        2. Click **Sign in with Google**
        3. Select your Google account
        4. Authorize AdRecon to access your basic profile information

        <Warning>
          New Google sign-ups require a valid Fanbasis purchase or admin provisioning. Existing users can sign in freely.
        </Warning>
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>

    After successful authentication, you'll be redirected to the main dashboard at `/app`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Explore the ad feed">
    The dashboard displays a live feed of active ad creatives from ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, and MaxWeb.

    **View options:**
    - **All Ads** - Browse the full database of 100k+ tracked creatives
    - **Saved Ads** - View ads you've bookmarked
    - **Project** - Filter by a specific project folder

    **Display modes:**
    - **Table view** - Compact rows with key metrics
    - **Grid view** - Visual gallery of creative images

    Click any ad card to open the **Ad Modal** with full details including:
    - Creative image and ad copy
    - Offer name and advertiser
    - Landing page URL
    - Performance metrics (days running, first/last seen)
    - Network and niche classification
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save your first ad">
    Found a winning creative? Save it for later reference.

    **Quick save from feed:**
    1. Click the **bookmark icon** on any ad card
    2. The ad is instantly added to your Saved Ads collection

    **Save from Ad Modal:**
    1. Click an ad to open the detail modal
    2. Click **Save Ad** in the modal header
    3. Optionally assign the ad to one or more projects
    4. Click **Save** to confirm

    <Tip>
      Use the **undo** link (appears for 5 seconds after saving) to quickly reverse the action if saved by mistake.
    </Tip>

    View all your saved ads by selecting **Saved Ads** from the feed scope dropdown.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a project">
    Organize your saved ads into project folders for different campaigns, clients, or niches.

    1. Click **Projects** in the top navigation
    2. Click **Create Project**
    3. Enter a project name (e.g., "Health Supplements Q1")
    4. Click **Create**

    **Add ads to a project:**
    - From the **Save Ad Modal**, check the project boxes when saving
    - From the **Saved Ads** feed, click **Edit Projects** on any ad card
    - Ads can belong to multiple projects simultaneously

    <Info>
      Project names are case-insensitive and must be unique across your account.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Capture a landing page">
    Use the **Page Ripper** to download a self-contained copy of any ad's landing page.

    1. Open an ad in the Ad Modal
    2. Click **Rip Landing Page** in the Capture panel
    3. Wait for the headless browser to capture the page (10-60 seconds)
    4. Download the ZIP file when capture completes

    The ZIP archive includes:
    - `page.html` - Self-contained SingleFile HTML
    - `assets/` - All images, CSS, JS, fonts, and media

    <Warning>
      Page Ripper cannot capture Meta platform URLs (Facebook, Instagram, Threads) due to platform restrictions. Rate limit: 10 captures per 15 minutes.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common workflows

### Research competitive creatives
1. Filter by **Niche** (e.g., Health) and **Network** (e.g., ClickBank)
2. Sort by **Days Running** to find proven winners
3. Open the **Ad Modal** to analyze ad copy and landing pages
4. Save interesting creatives to a research project

### Build a swipe file
1. Create projects for different verticals or clients
2. Browse the feed and save strong creatives
3. Assign saved ads to relevant projects
4. Capture landing pages for offline reference

### Monitor competitor activity
1. Use the **Search** filter to find ads by offer name or advertiser
2. Check **Last Seen** dates to track active campaigns
3. Save competitor ads to a monitoring project
4. Set up regular check-ins to spot new creatives
